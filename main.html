Monday Protocol A -- Outdoor sprints
Most effective option. Sprinting recruits maximum muscle mass = highest lactate output of any bodyweight exercise.

5 min easy walk warm-up
30 sec all-out sprint then 90 sec walk back to start
Repeat 4-8x depending on week
5 min easy walk cool-down

Total ~20 min. Start next interval only when HR drops to 118-138 on Garmin.
Wednesday Protocol B -- Bodyweight circuit
No equipment, no setup. Each interval is max-effort burpees for 30 seconds.

5 min warm-up (jumping jacks, arm circles, leg swings)
30 sec max burpees then 90 sec rest
Repeat 4-8x depending on week
5 min cool-down walk

Progression: once burpees feel manageable, escalate to squat jump + push-up + 5x mountain climbers within the 30-sec window.
Friday Protocol C -- Dumbbell thrusters
HIIT and resistance simultaneously. Thrusters involve both lower and upper body, maximizing muscle recruitment and lactate output.

5 min warm-up
30 sec max dumbbell thrusters at 30-35 lbs then 90 sec rest
Repeat 4-8x depending on week
5 min cool-down

Thruster = hold dumbbells at shoulders, squat to parallel, drive up and press overhead in one fluid movement.
The one thing that makes or breaks this: the 30-sec interval must be genuinely maximal. If Garmin does not show 167+ bpm by end of the interval, you did not produce enough lactate to trigger the BDNF signal. Check after every interval for the first 4 weeks.
